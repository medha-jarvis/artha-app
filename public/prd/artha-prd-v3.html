01 Executive Summary

Artha is India's first Personal Finance Operating System — a freemium, subscription-funded platform that evolves from wealth tracking into a complete financial life command centre. Phase by phase, it gives Indian investors: (1) a consolidated view of all their wealth, (2) institutional-grade analytics, (3) goal-based planning, (4) on-demand research concierge, and (5) access to curated financial products — all without conflicted advice or distribution commissions.

The starting point (MVP): Track all 9 major Indian asset classes — direct equity, mutual funds, EPF, PPF, SSY, NPS, ULIP, PMS, and fixed deposits — with zero-friction brokerage statement imports (7 brokers, auto-format recognition), XIRR/TWRR with institutional-grade methodology, benchmark comparison, and AI commentary.

The destination: An investment concierge for the AI era. Users can tag investments to life goals, get retirement corpus projections, commission institutional-grade stock research reports (concall analysis, forensic, valuation) at a fraction of brokerage cost, and access curated financial products — on a platform that is structurally conflict-free by design.

The structural moat: Revenue from subscriptions + research fees. Zero distribution commissions. Every recommendation on Artha is structurally incapable of being biased. This is the rarest thing in Indian fintech: honest, conflict-free financial intelligence.

02 Problem Statement

An Indian investor with a typical HNI portfolio manages 6–9 separate portals daily. Even with all portals open simultaneously, they cannot compute a single honest answer to:

"What is my true net worth today, and how is every rupee actually performing?"

This question is structurally unsolvable with today's fragmented tools.

The 7 Core Pains

1. Fragmentation
6–9 portals for a complete picture. Zerodha for stocks, AMFI/Kuvera for MF, EPFO portal for EPF, NPS CRA for NPS, insurer site for ULIP, PMS manager for PMS, bank app for FD. Investors give up checking.
2. No true net worth
Investable assets ≠ net worth. EPF, PPF, SSY (often ₹30–70L combined for a 40-year-old) are almost never in the same view as stocks and MF.
3. Incomparable returns
MF shows XIRR. EPF shows "8.25% declared." PMS shows "35% absolute since inception." These are not comparable. No app solves this.
4. PMS & ULIP blindspot
₹5L+ crore combined AUM. Zero consumer analytics tools. ULIP investors don't know their true return after charges. PMS investors don't know if they're beating the index net of fees.
5. Conflicted advice
INDmoney, ET Money, Groww earn 0.5–1% on product distribution. Their "best fund" recommendations are structurally biased. No honest, conflict-free intelligence tool exists.
6. No tax intelligence
LTCG threshold ₹1.25L. Most investors discover their tax liability only in March/April. Tax harvesting opportunities (save ₹10K–2L/year) are routinely missed.
7. No AI commentary
Raw numbers with zero interpretation. "Your PMS has underperformed Nifty500 by 4% over 3 years" is a sentence no Indian wealth app has ever sent to a user.

03 Market Research & Competitive Landscape

Key Competitors

INDmoney (Series D — most funded competitor)

Founded 2019, Ashish Kashyap (Ibibo/GoIbibo). Covers stocks, MF, US stocks, EPF (via EPFO credential sharing — security concern), NPS, credit cards. Uses Account Aggregator for bank data. Has evolved into a full broker/investing platform.

Weaknesses: Revenue entirely from commissions (conflicted). No PMS/ULIP tracking. UI cluttered with product promotions. Analytics lack depth — no TWRR, no benchmark analysis, no AI commentary. EPFO requires sharing credentials (users uncomfortable). Recommended products = highest commission products.

ET Money (Times Group)

Strong MF tracking + NPS. Launched "Genius Plan" ₹299/mo — first premium wealth tracker in India. Good XIRR for MF portfolios. Clean UI.

Weaknesses: Stock portfolio manual entry only. No PMS/ULIP/SSY tracking. Cross-asset benchmarking absent. Revenue still from MF distribution. Genius features feel thin for ₹299/mo price point.

Kuvera

Best-in-class MF analytics. Direct plans only (no commission). Strong tax harvesting feature. Good XIRR reporting. Loyal user base among serious MF investors.

Weaknesses: Only MF. Not a wealth tracker. No stocks, EPF, NPS, ULIP, PMS. A category winner in a deliberately narrow category. Cannot show net worth.

Safebox

Positioned as "India's most complete net worth tracker." Tracks MF, property, gold, FDs, bank accounts, insurance. SEBI registered. Family-focused.

Weaknesses: No PMS/NPS/ULIP/EPF tracking. Property valuation is manual/estimated. No IRR/TWRR computation. Limited analytics. New product — ecosystem is immature.

04 Product Vision & USP

"Your financial concierge for the AI era. Know your true wealth. Plan for every goal. Invest with institutional intelligence — without anyone trying to sell you something."

The Vision: Personal Finance OS

Artha is not just a tracker. It is the operating system for an Indian investor's financial life — evolving in phases from a wealth dashboard to a full financial command centre. The analogy: Bloomberg Terminal for Indian retail investors, at 1% of the cost, without the conflict of interest.

Phase 0–1: Know (Wealth Intelligence) — Consolidated net worth across all 9 asset classes. Zero-friction imports from 7 brokers. XIRR/TWRR/benchmark. AI commentary. Tax cockpit. Deep stock & MF analytics with 1D returns, momentum signals, rolling performance, normalised comparison charts.
Phase 2–3: Plan (Goal OS) — Define life goals (retirement, child education, home, vacation, emergency corpus). Tag existing investments to goals. See if each goal is on track. AI-generated gap analysis: "You need ₹2.1 Cr more for retirement — here's a monthly SIP plan to bridge it." Retirement corpus planner with inflation-adjusted projections.
Phase 3–4: Research (Investment Concierge) — On-demand institutional-grade research reports for stocks. Concall analysis, management credibility audit, forensic financial analysis, reverse DCF valuation, comprehensive research report. User places a request → makes a payment → receives a PDF report permanently saved to their account. This is their concierge for intelligent investing.
Phase 5: Marketplace (Curated Products) — Artha-curated, conflict-screened financial products. Not commission-driven promotion — a hand-picked shelf of instruments that pass Artha's quality bar. Revenue from flat referral fees, not % AUM commissions. Trust-preserving monetisation.

8 Core USPs

1. True Total Wealth OS
All 9 Indian asset classes. Zero exceptions. EPF + PPF + SSY + NPS + ULIP + PMS + Stocks + MF + FD. One honest number.
2. Zero-Friction Import
7 brokerage formats auto-recognised. Upload multiple files — duplicates removed automatically. Stocks and MF ready in seconds, not hours.
3. Institutional Analytics
XIRR, TWRR, 1D P&L, rolling returns, benchmark normalisation, momentum signals, contribution attribution — same depth as a professional portfolio management system.
4. Goal Intelligence
Tag investments to life goals. Real-time goal progress. AI corpus projections. "You are ₹82L short of your retirement goal — here's what to do." No Indian app does this end-to-end.
5. Research Concierge
Institutional stock research on demand. Forensic, valuation, concall, management credibility — at ₹499–₹1,999/report. Permanently yours. No analyst conflicts.
6. Conflict-Free by Design
Subscriptions + research fees only. Zero distribution commissions. Structurally incapable of biased advice — not just by policy.
7. AI Commentary
Weekly: "Your PMS earned 9.2% vs Nifty500 14.1% — ₹2.1L fee drag for negative alpha. Is active management worth it?" No Indian app sends this message.
8. PMS & ULIP Truth
True XIRR on ULIP after all charges. PMS fee drain vs alpha. "Your ULIP breaks even in year 9." The honest analysis your agent won't give — because they earned commission on the sale.

10 User Stories & Acceptance Criteria

Authentication & Onboarding

US-001 · Auth
As a new user, I want to sign up with Google OAuth in one click
so that I don't need to remember another password
Acceptance Criteria
  • Google OAuth button on signup and login page
  • On first Google login, user profile created with name + email from Google
  • User directed to profile setup (tax bracket, risk profile) before dashboard
  • Existing email-password users can link Google account later in settings
US-002 · Onboarding
As a new user, I want a guided first-run experience that gets me to my first insight in under 5 minutes
so that I immediately understand the product's value
Acceptance Criteria
  • After signup: 3-step wizard (profile → add first holding → see dashboard)
  • Progress bar visible throughout wizard
  • Wizard can be skipped, but nudge appears on dashboard until first holding added
  • First "aha moment" (XIRR shown) must occur before wizard ends
  • Time from signup to first XIRR displayed < 5 minutes (P50)

Portfolio Management

US-010 · Stock Import
As a Zerodha user, I want to upload my P&L CSV and have all my stock trades automatically imported
so that I don't have to enter 200 trades manually
Acceptance Criteria
  • Accept Zerodha P&L CSV format (standard columns: symbol, trade_date, trade_type, quantity, price)
  • Auto-detect column headers even if slightly renamed
  • Show import preview: N trades, N symbols, date range, before confirmation
  • Detect and flag duplicate transactions (same date + symbol + quantity + price)
  • Show import success summary: "X trades imported across Y symbols"
  • Handle partial failures gracefully: import valid rows, show errors for invalid rows
US-011 · XIRR Display
As an investor, I want to see XIRR for each of my holdings and my total portfolio
so that I can compare performance across different investments on a common basis
Acceptance Criteria
  • XIRR shown for every holding with at least 2 transactions (entry + current value)
  • XIRR shown per asset class (aggregated across all holdings in that class)
  • Total portfolio XIRR computed across all cash flows
  • XIRR displayed as annualized percentage to 1 decimal (e.g., "14.2%")
  • For holdings with insufficient data: show "–" with tooltip explaining why
  • XIRR recalculated automatically when new transactions are added or prices update
US-015 · PMS Entry
As a PMS investor, I want to see my true XIRR after all fees and compare it to the benchmark
so that I can objectively evaluate if my PMS manager is worth the fee
Acceptance Criteria
  • PMS form captures: provider, strategy, investment date, amount, current value, benchmark, fee structure
  • Multiple cash flows supported (add/withdrawal dates + amounts for accurate XIRR)
  • Show "PMS Verdict" card: XIRR vs benchmark TWRR, fees paid (₹), net alpha in ₹
  • Benchmark TWRR loaded from benchmark_data table (daily Nifty500 TRI values)
  • Fee drain calculated as: (fees paid) minus (alpha generated in ₹). Negative = PMS destroying value

12 Metrics Engine

Three metrics, three purposes — never confused

XIRR = what your money earned (cash-flow adjusted). TWRR = how the manager performed (cash-flow neutral). Absolute Return = simple ₹ and % gain. Each answers a different question. Artha shows all three, labeled clearly.

XIRR — Implementation Detail

  • Algorithm: Newton-Raphson iteration solving: Σ [Cᵢ / (1+r)^(dᵢ/365)] = 0, where Cᵢ = cash flow on day dᵢ, r = XIRR
  • Implementation: scipy.optimize.newton() in Python serverless function. JavaScript fallback: xirr npm package.
  • Cash flow convention: Investments = negative cash flows. Withdrawals/redemptions = positive cash flows. Terminal value (current price × units) = positive cash flow dated today.
  • Edge cases: Single transaction → display simple annualized return instead. All transactions same date → display absolute return. XIRR computation failure → display "N/A" with tooltip.
  • Performance: Computed server-side. Cached in portfolio_metrics table. Cache invalidated on new transaction or price update. Cache TTL: 24 hours for positions unchanged.

TWRR — Implementation Detail

  • Algorithm: Chain-linked sub-period returns. For each sub-period between cash flows: rₙ = (V_end) / (V_start + CF_inflow) − 1. TWRR = ∏(1 + rₙ) − 1
  • Data requirement: Daily NAV history. For MF: fetched from AMFI. For NPS: user-entered current NAV (monthly granularity acceptable). For PMS: user-entered monthly valuations.
  • When to use: MF, PMS, NPS only. EPF/PPF/SSY use XIRR (no daily NAV). Stocks use XIRR.
  • Benchmark TWRR: Nifty50 TRI, Nifty500 TRI, category average stored in benchmark_data table. Loaded from NSE/external source daily.

Tax Computation — LTCG/STCG Engine

  • Lot tracking: Each buy transaction = one lot. Sells reduce lots in FIFO order.
  • Equity (Stocks + Equity MF): Holding period <12 months: STCG at 15%. ≥12 months: LTCG at 10% on gains above ₹1.25L threshold.
  • Debt MF (post March 2023): All gains taxed at slab rate regardless of holding period.
  • LTCG threshold: ₹1.25L cumulative per FY (April–March). Artha tracks running total from April 1 each year.
  • Tax harvesting logic: Identify lots with LTCG > remaining threshold room. Find offsetting loss lots in same category. Net savings = min(LTCG in lot, loss in offsetting lot) × 10%.

Import Flow — UX Design

Step 1: Upload
Drag-and-drop or file picker. Accepts CSV, XLSX, PDF. Multiple files allowed in one session.
Step 2: Auto-Detect
System fingerprints the file header and announces: "Detected: Zerodha P&L — 847 transactions." User can override if wrong.
Step 3: Preview & Confirm
Scrollable preview of parsed transactions with color-coded BUY/SELL. Any unrecognised rows highlighted for manual review.
Step 4: Dedup & Import
System checks against existing transactions (by ISIN + date + qty + price). Shows: "12 duplicates skipped, 835 new transactions imported." Commit button. XIRR refreshes immediately.

Deduplication Logic

  • Primary key for dedup: (symbol/ISIN, trade_date, trade_type, quantity, price) — all 5 must match to be a duplicate
  • Fuzzy match: Same symbol, same date, quantity within 1 unit — flagged as "probable duplicate", user confirms
  • Multi-file import: User can upload Zerodha 2022, Zerodha 2023, Zerodha 2024 in one session — system merges and dedups across all three
  • Cross-broker dedup: If same stock appears in both Zerodha and Groww files (e.g., moved broker), system flags for user confirmation
